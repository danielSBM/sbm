## How do pay-per-result models work as alternatives to traditional agency pricing?

Pay-per-result digital marketing charges businesses only for measurable outcomes like qualified leads, new customers, or revenue generated rather than time-based retainer fees. Waco businesses using this model typically pay $75-$250 per qualified lead or 15-25% of attributed revenue, eliminating upfront risk while aligning agency incentives with client success.

**Performance-based pricing requires sophisticated tracking infrastructure** to accurately attribute results to specific marketing activities. Advanced call tracking, CRM integration, and multi-touch attribution systems ensure Waco businesses pay only for genuinely qualified prospects rather than superficial metrics like website visits or social media engagement.

The model works through clearly defined success metrics established before campaign launch. For Waco HVAC companies, qualified leads might include homeowners requesting service estimates with verified contact information. Restaurants could pay for actual reservation bookings or catering inquiries. Healthcare practices might compensate for new patient appointments scheduled and confirmed.

**Risk distribution shifts dramatically** under pay-per-result structures. Traditional retainer models require Waco businesses to fund marketing experiments with no guaranteed outcomes. Performance-based approaches transfer financial risk to marketing providers who profit only when delivering measurable business growth.

Typical pricing structures include:
- **Lead Generation**: $50-$200 per qualified lead depending on industry value
- **Revenue Share**: 10-20% of attributed new customer revenue
- **Appointment Setting**: $75-$150 per confirmed appointment
- **E-commerce**: 15-25% of attributed online sales

Quality controls prevent gaming through minimum qualification standards. Lead generation campaigns require prospects to meet specific criteria — geographic location, budget capacity, genuine purchase intent — before triggering payment obligations.

**Campaign optimization accelerates** under performance models since agencies profit directly from improved results. Traditional retainer relationships can persist for months with declining performance, while pay-per-result providers must continuously optimize to maintain revenue streams.
