## What Metrics Should Atlanta Businesses Track for Online Advertising?

**Atlanta businesses should focus on cost per acquisition (CPA), customer lifetime value (LTV), and return on ad spend (ROAS) as primary metrics, with successful campaigns maintaining LTV:CPA ratios above 3:1.**

Tracking the right metrics determines advertising success or failure. Many Atlanta businesses focus on vanity metrics like impressions or clicks while ignoring profitability indicators.

### Essential Performance Metrics for Atlanta Businesses

**Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**
Your total advertising cost divided by customers acquired. Atlanta service businesses should target:
- Home services: $125-$280 CPA
- Professional services: $180-$450 CPA
- Healthcare: $95-$320 CPA
- Retail: $32-$89 CPA

**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**
Total revenue generated per customer over their relationship with your business. Atlanta businesses typically see:
- B2B services: $5,000-$35,000 LTV
- Home services: $2,400-$8,500 LTV
- Healthcare: $1,800-$12,000 LTV
- Retail: $180-$650 LTV

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**
Revenue generated divided by advertising cost. Profitable Atlanta campaigns maintain:
- Minimum acceptable ROAS: 3:1 ($3 revenue per $1 spent)
- Good ROAS: 5:1
- Excellent ROAS: 8:1 or higher

### Advanced Metrics for Atlanta Market Analysis

**Geographic Performance Breakdown**
Track performance by Atlanta neighborhoods and surrounding cities to optimize targeting:
- Buckhead: Higher CPA, higher LTV
- Midtown: Tech-savvy audience, social media responsive
- North suburbs: Family-oriented, home services demand

Atlanta's traffic patterns and work schedules affect advertising performance:

Atlanta's diverse economy requires customized strategies. Agencies using identical approaches for all clients lack market sophistication.
