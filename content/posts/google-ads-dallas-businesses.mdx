## Common Google Ads Mistakes Dallas Companies Make

Dallas businesses frequently make campaign optimization errors that drain budgets while generating poor-quality leads. Understanding these pitfalls helps companies avoid wasted spend and improve ROI.

### Targeting Too Broad Geographic Areas

**Metroplex-wide targeting errors:** Many Dallas companies make the mistake of targeting the entire DFW metroplex (20+ cities) with identical campaigns. This approach wastes budget on low-value traffic from distant suburbs while underutilizing high-converting urban areas.

**Keyword geographic modifiers:** Using only "Dallas" as a geographic modifier misses valuable traffic from users searching "near me" or specific suburb names. Companies should include variations like "Dallas TX," "DFW," and nearby city names (Plano, Irving, Richardson) to capture broader relevant traffic.

### Ignoring Negative Keywords

Dallas's diverse economy creates search query overlap between industries. A Dallas IT services company might waste budget on searches for "IT training" or "IT jobs" rather than "IT support" or "managed IT services."

**Industry-specific negative keywords:** Energy companies should exclude terms like "energy drinks," "renewable energy jobs," and "energy efficiency rebates" to avoid irrelevant clicks. Healthcare practices must exclude "careers," "jobs," and "insurance" to focus on patient acquisition.

### Poor Landing Page Relevance

**Generic landing pages:** Many Dallas businesses drive Google Ads traffic to generic homepages rather than service-specific landing pages. This approach reduces conversion rates by 40-60% compared to targeted landing pages that match search intent.

**Lack of Dallas-specific content:** Landing pages should reference Dallas markets, local landmarks, and community connections to build trust with local searchers. Generic content performs poorly compared to pages that demonstrate local market knowledge.
