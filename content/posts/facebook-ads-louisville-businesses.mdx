## Common Louisville Facebook Advertising Mistakes to Avoid

Louisville businesses consistently make preventable Facebook advertising mistakes that drain budgets and limit results. Understanding these common errors enables strategic avoidance and competitive advantage development.

**Geographic Targeting Errors:**

Over-broad geographic targeting wastes budget on irrelevant audiences. Campaigns targeting "Kentucky" or "United States" dilute Louisville-specific messaging and increase costs. Conversely, overly restrictive targeting to Louisville city limits excludes valuable suburban and Southern Indiana audiences.

Optimal Louisville geographic targeting typically includes Jefferson County, plus portions of Oldham, Bullitt, Nelson, and Shelby counties in Kentucky, along with Clark and Floyd counties in Indiana. This approach captures 89% of Louisville's customer base while maintaining message relevance.

**Audience Overlap Issues:**

Multiple campaigns targeting overlapping Louisville audiences create internal competition and increased costs. Facebook's auction system forces your campaigns to compete against each other, driving up prices unnecessarily.

Use Facebook's audience overlap tool to identify conflicts between campaigns. Campaigns with 25%+ audience overlap should be consolidated or have exclusion targeting implemented to prevent internal competition.

**Budget Allocation Mistakes:**

Equal budget distribution across all campaigns ignores performance differences and wastes money on underperforming segments. Louisville businesses should allocate 60-70% of budgets to proven high-performing campaigns while testing new approaches with smaller allocations.

Campaign budget optimization (CBO) works better than ad set budgets for Louisville businesses running multiple audience tests. CBO automatically distributes budget to best-performing audience segments within campaigns.

### Creative Rotation and Testing Errors

Louisville audiences develop ad fatigue when exposed to identical creative assets repeatedly. Creative rotation every 2-3 weeks prevents performance degradation and maintains engagement rates.

Static creative testing approaches limit optimization opportunities. Successful Louisville campaigns test multiple creative variations simultaneously, measuring performance across different audience segments and optimization objectives.

Poor mobile optimization creates significant opportunity costs since 84% of Louisville Facebook users access the platform primarily through mobile devices. Creative assets must display effectively on mobile screens with clear, readable text and compelling visuals.
