## Measuring Success: What Metrics Matter Most

**Answer: The three most important metrics for measuring online customer acquisition success in San Francisco are customer acquisition cost (target: under 30% of customer lifetime value), conversion rate by traffic source (benchmark: 3-5%), and return on ad spend (target: 4:1 minimum).**

### Primary Success Metrics

**1. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
- Definition: Total marketing spend divided by new customers acquired
- San Francisco benchmark: $180-$540 depending on industry
- Target: Less than 30% of customer lifetime value
- Track by: Channel, campaign, time period

**2. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**
- Definition: Revenue generated divided by advertising cost
- San Francisco average: 3.8:1 across all industries
- Target: Minimum 4:1 for sustainable growth
- Track by: Campaign, keyword group, audience segment

**3. Conversion Rate**
- Definition: Percentage of visitors who become customers
- San Francisco average: 3.4% (higher than national 2.8%)
- Target: 5%+ for optimized campaigns
- Track by: Traffic source, landing page, device type

### Secondary Success Metrics

**4. Lead Quality Score**
- Sales-qualified lead percentage
- Time from lead to customer
- Average deal size by source

**5. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**
- Total revenue per customer over relationship
- Retention rate by acquisition channel
- Referral rate from acquired customers

### Tracking and Reporting Best Practices

**Monthly performance review should include:**
- Channel performance comparison
- Trending metrics over 3-6 month periods
- Customer acquisition cost trends
- ROI by marketing activity

**Quarterly strategic review should include:**
- Market share analysis within San Francisco
- Competitive positioning assessment
- Channel mix optimization
- Budget reallocation based on performance

The key is focusing on metrics that directly tie to business growth rather than vanity metrics like website visits or social media followers.
