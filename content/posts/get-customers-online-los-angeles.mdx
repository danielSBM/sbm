## Measuring Success: KPIs That Matter for Los Angeles Customer Acquisition

**The only metric that matters is cost per acquired customer, but Los Angeles businesses need to track leading indicators to optimize campaigns before budget waste occurs.** Focus on metrics that predict customer acquisition.

### Primary KPIs

1. **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Total marketing spend ÷ customers acquired
2. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**: Average revenue per customer over relationship duration  
3. **CLV:CAC Ratio**: Should be 3:1 minimum, 5:1 ideal for Los Angeles market
4. **Time to Payback**: How long to recover acquisition investment

### Secondary KPIs

1. **Lead-to-customer conversion rate**: Percentage of leads that become paying customers
2. **Cost per qualified lead**: Marketing spend ÷ qualified leads generated
3. **Channel attribution**: Which channels generate highest-value customers
4. **Geographic performance**: Customer acquisition cost by neighborhood/zip code

### Los Angeles-Specific Metrics

1. **Drive time impact**: Correlation between customer distance and lifetime value
2. **Seasonal variation**: How LA weather and events affect acquisition
3. **Competitive displacement**: Customers won from specific competitors
4. **Review velocity**: Rate of review generation per customer acquired

The key insight: Los Angeles businesses need location-based attribution. A customer from Manhattan Beach might have 2x higher lifetime value than one from downtown, even for the same service. Track performance by zip code, not just channel.
