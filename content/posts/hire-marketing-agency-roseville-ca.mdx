## How to Evaluate Agency Proposals and Contracts?

Agency proposals reveal priorities, capabilities, and potential red flags before you sign any contracts. Roseville businesses should evaluate proposals using specific criteria that separate serious partners from marketing vendors.

### Proposal Red Flags to Avoid

**Vague Strategy Descriptions**
Proposals mentioning "comprehensive digital marketing strategies" without specific tactics, timelines, or success metrics indicate agencies that haven't invested time understanding your business. Quality proposals include detailed analysis of your current market position, competitive landscape, and specific growth opportunities.

**Activity-Based Success Metrics**  
Avoid agencies proposing success measurement through website visits, social media followers, or email open rates. These vanity metrics don't correlate with business growth. Demand proposals focused on customer acquisition, sales qualified leads, and revenue impact.

**Unrealistic Timeline Expectations**
Proposals promising immediate results (30-60 days) or requiring 12+ month commitments both signal problems. Legitimate marketing results require 90-120 days to materialize and optimize, while performance-confident agencies offer flexible terms rather than lengthy lock-in periods.

**Generic Market Analysis**
Proposals that don't mention Roseville's specific business environment, competitor landscape, or local customer behavior indicate cookie-cutter approaches. Your agency should demonstrate understanding of Roseville's position between Sacramento and Lake Tahoe markets.

### Contract Terms That Protect Your Business

**Performance Guarantees and Exit Clauses**
Contracts should specify measurable performance minimums (qualified leads delivered, customer acquisition costs achieved, revenue impact generated) with clear consequences for underperformance. Avoid agencies unwilling to provide 30-60 day exit clauses if results don't materialize.

**Transparent Reporting Requirements**
Demand monthly reporting that includes customer acquisition costs, lead quality metrics, and direct revenue attribution. Contracts should guarantee access to all advertising accounts, analytics platforms, and campaign data regardless of relationship status.

**Intellectual Property Protection**
Ensure contracts specify that creative assets, customer data, and campaign strategies developed during the partnership remain your business property. Some agencies claim ownership of marketing assets to complicate transitions.

## How to Monitor and Measure Agency Performance?

Effective agency oversight requires systematic monitoring of customer acquisition metrics rather than marketing activity reports. Roseville businesses should establish measurement frameworks that provide real-time visibility into campaign performance and business impact.

### Essential Tracking Metrics

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel**
Track the total cost to acquire each new customer through different marketing channels (Google Ads, Facebook, SEO, etc.). This metric reveals which strategies deliver the most efficient growth for your Roseville business.

Calculate CAC by dividing total marketing spend by new customers acquired within each channel during specific time periods. Include both agency fees and advertising costs for accurate measurement.

**Lead Quality Scoring**
Not all leads convert equally. Establish scoring criteria based on budget, timeline, and fit with your ideal customer profile. Track what percentage of agency-generated leads meet quality thresholds.

**Monthly Recurring Revenue Impact**
For service businesses, measure how agency-generated customers contribute to monthly recurring revenue growth. This metric demonstrates long-term value creation beyond initial customer acquisition.

**Local Market Share Growth**
Monitor your business's visibility and market share within Roseville using local search rankings, customer survey data, and competitive analysis. Effective marketing should increase local market presence measurably.

### Reporting Requirements

**Weekly Performance Dashboards**
Demand weekly reports showing customer acquisition numbers, costs, and quality metrics. Monthly reporting creates too much lag time to address underperforming campaigns quickly.

**Real-Time Campaign Access**  
Maintain direct access to all advertising accounts, analytics platforms, and conversion tracking systems. Never rely solely on agency-provided reports without verification capabilities.

**Monthly Business Impact Reviews**
Monthly meetings should focus on business growth metrics rather than marketing activity reports. Review revenue attribution, customer lifetime value trends, and market share development.

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Attention

**Declining Lead Quality Over Time**
If lead quality degrades while volume increases, your agency may be optimizing for quantity rather than business value. Address this immediately to prevent sales team frustration and wasted follow-up time.

**Increasing Customer Acquisition Costs**
CAC should generally decrease over time as campaigns optimize and agencies learn your market. Rising costs indicate poor optimization or market saturation requiring strategy adjustment.

**Lack of Transparency in Reporting**
Agencies providing high-level summaries without detailed performance data may be hiding poor results. Demand granular reporting with direct platform access for verification.
