## What's the difference between traditional marketing agencies and pay-per-result advertising?

Traditional marketing agencies charge monthly retainers of $3,000-15,000 regardless of results delivered, while pay-per-result advertising models only charge when specific outcomes occur—typically qualified leads, sales, or customer acquisitions. This fundamental difference shifts financial risk from Seattle small businesses to the advertising provider.

Retainer-based agencies in Seattle typically require 6-12 month contracts with fees covering strategy development, campaign management, creative production, and reporting. A typical Seattle digital marketing agency charges $5,000-8,000 monthly for comprehensive services, totaling $30,000-48,000 annually before advertising spend. These agencies justify retainers by emphasizing ongoing optimization and strategic planning.

Pay-per-result models eliminate upfront costs and monthly minimums. Instead, businesses pay predetermined amounts for each qualified customer delivered. For example, a Seattle restaurant might pay $35 per confirmed reservation, while a B2B software company pays $150 per qualified sales meeting. This pricing structure aligns agency incentives with business outcomes rather than activity metrics.
