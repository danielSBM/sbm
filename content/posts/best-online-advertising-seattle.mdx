## What Metrics Should Seattle Small Businesses Track for Online Advertising Success?

Customer acquisition cost (CAC) and lifetime value (LTV) provide the most accurate measures of online advertising success for Seattle small businesses, with target CAC ratios of 1:3 to 1:5 depending on industry. Tracking platform-specific vanity metrics like impressions and clicks wastes analysis time without indicating actual business impact.

### Essential Performance Metrics

Successful Seattle businesses focus on revenue-generating metrics rather than engagement statistics that don't correlate with actual customer acquisition. The high cost of customer acquisition in Seattle's competitive market makes precise measurement critical for campaign profitability.

**Primary Revenue Metrics**
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Total advertising spend divided by new customers acquired
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: Revenue generated divided by advertising investment
- **Lifetime Value (LTV)**: Average customer value over entire relationship duration
- **Conversion Rate**: Percentage of ad clicks that result in desired actions
- **Cost Per Qualified Lead**: Advertising cost for leads meeting specific criteria

**Secondary Optimization Metrics**
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Indicates ad relevance and targeting accuracy
- **Cost-Per-Click (CPC)**: Helps optimize bidding strategies and budget allocation
- **Quality Score**: Google's assessment of ad relevance affecting costs and placement
- **Attribution Analysis**: Understanding which touchpoints contribute to conversions
- **Geographic Performance**: Comparing results across Seattle neighborhoods and surrounding areas
