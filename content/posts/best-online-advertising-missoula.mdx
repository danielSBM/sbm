## Measuring Success: Key Performance Indicators for Missoula Businesses

Digital advertising success requires tracking metrics that directly correlate with business growth and revenue generation. Missoula small businesses should focus on conversion-based KPIs rather than vanity metrics.

### Primary Success Metrics

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
The total cost to acquire one new customer through digital advertising. Missoula businesses should track CAC by channel to identify the most efficient advertising platforms.

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):**
Revenue generated for every dollar invested in advertising. Successful campaigns typically achieve 3:1-5:1 ROAS, meaning every $1 spent generates $3-5 in revenue.

**Lifetime Customer Value (LCV):**
The total revenue expected from a customer relationship. Comparing LCV to CAC determines campaign profitability and acceptable acquisition costs.

**Conversion Rate by Traffic Source:**
The percentage of website visitors who complete desired actions (purchases, appointments, contact forms). This metric reveals which advertising channels attract the highest-quality prospects.

### Secondary Tracking Metrics

**Click-Through Rates (CTR):**
The percentage of ad impressions resulting in clicks. Higher CTRs indicate compelling ad creative and relevant audience targeting.

**Cost-Per-Click (CPC):**
The average amount paid for each ad click. Monitoring CPC trends helps optimize bidding strategies and budget allocation.

**Quality Score (Google Ads):**
Google's rating of ad relevance, landing page experience, and expected click-through rate. Higher quality scores reduce costs and improve ad positioning.

**Engagement Metrics (Social Media):**
Likes, comments, shares, and video completions indicate audience resonance with advertising creative and messaging.

Missoula businesses should review performance data weekly and make optimization decisions based on 30-day trends rather than daily fluctuations. Successful campaigns require consistent monitoring and strategic adjustments based on performance data.
