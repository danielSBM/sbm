## What Metrics Should Flagstaff Small Businesses Track?

Flagstaff small businesses must track cost per acquisition (CPA), customer lifetime value (CLV), and return on ad spend (ROAS) as primary metrics, with CPA targets varying by industry from $18 for restaurants to $85 for professional services. Secondary metrics include geographic conversion rates, seasonal performance variations, and campaign attribution across multiple touchpoints.

Cost per acquisition represents the total advertising investment required to generate one paying customer. This metric accounts for all advertising costs including platform fees, creative development, and management expenses. Flagstaff businesses should calculate CPA monthly and adjust campaigns when costs exceed industry benchmarks or customer lifetime value thresholds.

Customer lifetime value measures total revenue generated per customer over their entire relationship with the business. For Flagstaff's seasonal economy, CLV calculations must account for visit frequency, seasonal behavior changes, and referral generation. Tourism businesses often see single-visit customers while local service providers develop multi-year relationships.

Return on ad spend provides immediate campaign performance feedback. ROAS calculation divides total revenue generated by total advertising costs, expressed as a ratio (3:1 means $3 revenue per $1 advertising investment). Successful Flagstaff campaigns typically achieve 3:1 minimum ROAS, with 5:1+ indicating excellent performance.
