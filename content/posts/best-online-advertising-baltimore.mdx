## Measuring Online Advertising Success: Baltimore Business Metrics

**Successful Baltimore businesses track customer lifetime value (CLV) rather than just cost-per-click, with top performers achieving CLV-to-acquisition-cost ratios of 4:1 or higher within 90 days.** Proper measurement separates profitable campaigns from vanity metrics that waste advertising budgets.

### Essential Metrics for Baltimore Small Businesses

**Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** measures the total cost to acquire one new customer, including advertising spend and management fees. Baltimore service businesses should target CPA at 15-25% of customer lifetime value, while retail businesses can sustain higher acquisition costs due to repeat purchases.

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** calculates revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising. Baltimore restaurants typically achieve 3:1 ROAS on Facebook advertising during peak seasons, while B2B services may accept 2:1 ROAS due to higher customer lifetime values.

**Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** represents total revenue expected from a customer relationship. A Baltimore fitness studio with $89 monthly memberships and 18-month average retention achieves $1,602 CLV, justifying acquisition costs up to $400 per new member.
