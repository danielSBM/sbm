## Performance Metrics That Actually Matter for St. George Businesses

Revenue-generating metrics like customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, and return on ad spend matter more than vanity metrics like website traffic, social media followers, or email open rates. St. George businesses should demand reporting that directly correlates advertising spend with new customer acquisition.

### Primary Performance Indicators

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: The total cost to acquire one new customer, including advertising spend and management fees. Sustainable CAC should be 3-5x lower than average customer lifetime value. St. George service businesses typically maintain CAC between $75-$300 depending on industry.

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: Revenue generated divided by advertising costs. Healthy ROAS targets vary by industry but should exceed 3:1 for most St. George businesses. E-commerce companies often achieve 4-8:1 ROAS, while service businesses target 5-12:1 ratios.

**Qualified Lead Percentage**: Percentage of generated leads that meet specific qualification criteria like budget, timeline, and geographic location. High-performing campaigns in St. George achieve 65-85% qualified lead rates through precise targeting.

**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**: Total revenue generated by average customer over entire relationship. Understanding LTV enables appropriate CAC investment levels and campaign optimization priorities.

### Secondary Optimization Metrics

**Conversion Rate by Traffic Source**: Different advertising channels produce varying conversion rates. Google Ads typically convert at 8-15% for St. George service businesses, while Facebook advertising converts at 3-8% depending on targeting precision.

**Geographic Performance**: Analysis of customer acquisition performance by location within greater St. George area. This data optimizes geographic targeting and identifies expansion opportunities.

**Seasonal Performance Trends**: Understanding how advertising performance varies by season enables budget allocation optimization and campaign timing improvements.

### Metrics to Ignore

**Website Traffic**: High traffic without customer acquisition indicates targeting or conversion optimization problems. Focus on qualified visitor quality over total volume.

**Social Media Followers**: Follower count rarely correlates with revenue generation. Prioritize engagement rates and conversion tracking over vanity metrics.

**Email Open Rates**: Open rates depend heavily on subject line tactics and list quality rather than business growth potential. Focus on click-through rates and conversion percentages instead.
