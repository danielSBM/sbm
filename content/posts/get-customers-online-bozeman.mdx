## How to Measure Success in Online Customer Acquisition

Success in online customer acquisition for Bozeman businesses should be measured by three core metrics: cost per acquired customer, customer lifetime value, and return on advertising spend (ROAS), with successful campaigns typically achieving 3:1 to 5:1 ROAS depending on industry and average transaction values.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) That Matter

**Primary Metrics:**
1. **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** = Total marketing spend ÷ Number of new customers acquired
2. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** = Average purchase value × Purchase frequency × Customer lifespan
3. **Return on Advertising Spend (ROAS)** = Revenue generated ÷ Advertising investment

**Secondary Metrics:**
- Lead-to-customer conversion rate by traffic source
- Average time from first contact to purchase decision
- Repeat purchase rate within 12 months
- Referral rate from acquired customers
- Geographic distribution of acquired customers

### Attribution Modeling for Multi-Channel Campaigns

Most customers interact with multiple marketing touchpoints before purchasing. Accurate measurement requires attribution modeling that credits all contributing channels rather than only the final interaction.

**Common Customer Journeys:**

Single-channel attribution undervalues awareness-building activities while overvaluing final-touch interactions. Businesses should implement tracking systems that measure the complete customer acquisition journey.
