## How to Evaluate Marketing Agency Results in Duluth

Measuring marketing agency performance requires tracking business outcomes, not marketing activities. Duluth businesses must focus on metrics that directly impact revenue and growth rather than vanity metrics that look impressive but don't drive profits.

### Primary Performance Indicators:

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Total marketing investment divided by new customers acquired. Duluth businesses should target CAC below 20% of customer lifetime value.

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):** Revenue generated divided by advertising spend. Profitable Duluth businesses typically achieve 3:1 ROAS minimum, with mature campaigns reaching 5:1 or higher.

**Customer Lifetime Value (CLV):** Total revenue generated per customer over their relationship with your business. Duluth's smaller market makes customer retention especially critical.

**Attribution Accuracy:** Percentage of customers properly tracked from first touchpoint to final conversion. Agencies should achieve 85%+ attribution accuracy across all channels.

### Secondary Metrics That Matter:

**Lead Quality Score:** Percentage of leads that become paying customers. Quality matters more than quantity in Duluth's competitive market.

**Sales Cycle Impact:** How marketing affects the time from first contact to purchase decision. Effective campaigns should reduce sales cycles by 15-25%.

**Market Share Growth:** Quarterly increases in local market penetration, measured through brand searches and competitive analysis.

### Red Flag Metrics to Ignore:

**Social Media Followers:** Follower counts don't correlate with revenue for most Duluth businesses.

**Website Traffic:** Traffic without conversion optimization is expensive entertainment, not marketing.

**Brand Awareness Surveys:** Awareness doesn't pay bills unless it converts to customers.

**Engagement Rates:** Likes and comments mean nothing if they don't generate sales.
